Step 1 of 3 — Plan it on paper first

What should your AI workflow do?

Workflows shine on repeated, multi-step, structured tasks — the kind of work you'd do the same way every time. One-off creative tasks ("write me a poem") are better handled by just asking an AI directly. Before you start dragging boxes around, get clear on the four pieces every workflow needs.

1 Input — what triggers it?
A user click? A scheduled time? An uploaded file? An email arriving?
2 Sub-tasks — what happens, in order?
Break the job into 3–5 steps an AI assistant could do one at a time.
3 Tools — which tool handles each step?
An LLM? A web search? A specific API like Gmail or Whisper?
4 Output — what does it deliver, and to whom?
A Slack message? A Google Doc? An email? A spoken response?
